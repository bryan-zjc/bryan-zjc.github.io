<small>With the emerging Internet of Things (IoT) and vehicle-road-cloud integration system (VRCIS) technologies, coordinating connected and automated vehicles (CAVs) and traffic signals is becoming a practical solution to further enhance traffic efficiency. However, current studies still have limitations. First, there is a domain mismatch between CAV trajectory planning and signal optimization. This mismatch requires separate modeling of trajectory planning and signal optimization, which greatly reduces global optimality. Second, previous studies are not applicable to the actual mixed traffic environment since they mostly simplify the behavior of human-driven vehicles (HVs) without considering queuing and stop-and-go maneuvers. Therefore, we propose a novel multivehicles and signal coordination (MVSC) planner to solve the limitations via the following designs: 1) joint optimization is achieved via formulating in the spatial domain, unifying CAV’s planning domain with the traffic signal optimizing domain; 2) a parallel algorithm is designed for the adaptation to the numbers of CAVs; this algorithm is based on alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), making full use of IoT and VRCIS; and 3) HV queuing and stop-and-go behaviors are considered in our modeling. Simulation results show that the proposed MVSC planner can enhance efficiency and ecology by 23.60% and 15.63%, respectively. At CAV’s penetration rate of 40% and V/C ratio of 0.75, the proposed planner shows its full potential in performance enhancement. The average computation time of the parallel computing approach is only within 10 ms, which confirms the real-time implementation capability.</small>
